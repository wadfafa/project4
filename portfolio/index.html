About My Work

For the past 4 years, I've been immersed in the world of film photography. This journey began after inheriting my grandfather's cameras and thousands of film photos. I've carefully scanned and digitized over four thousand of his photographs to preserve his legacy for generations to come.

I shoot primarily with his Olympus Pen FV, a solid metal half-frame 35mm film camera that produces beautifully grainy images with a unique character. My work focuses on natural landscapes and candid moments with friends, documenting life through my perspective whether indoors or in vast open spaces.

Through film photography, I've discovered a way to connect with my grandfather's artistic vision while developing my own. Each roll of film is a deliberate exercise in patience and observation, resulting in images that feel more intentional and meaningful than what I could achieve digitally.

Why Film is Superior

In a world dominated by digital photography, film offers a unique quality that can't be replicated. The grain, the colors, the dynamic range, and the intentionality required all contribute to a distinctive aesthetic that stands apart from digital imagery.

Film forces you to slow down, to think about each shot before pressing the shutter. This deliberate approach results in more thoughtful compositions and a deeper connection to the subjects being photographed. When you only have 24 or 36 frames on a roll, each exposure becomes precious and considered.

The rendering of light in film photography has a special quality â€“ the way highlights gently roll off, how shadows retain detail, and how colors blend organically creates images with depth and character that digital sensors still struggle to match. Film stocks each have their own personality, rendering scenes with distinctive palettes that become part of your artistic signature.

My Film Process

Shooting

Each frame is carefully composed and exposed, considering the unique characteristics of the film stock being used.

Developing

Films are developed with precision, either at home or by trusted professional labs that understand the art of film processing.

Scanning

High-resolution scanning preserves the unique qualities of film while creating digital files for sharing and printing.

Printing

Selected images are printed on archival paper to create tangible, lasting physical artifacts of the photographic process.
